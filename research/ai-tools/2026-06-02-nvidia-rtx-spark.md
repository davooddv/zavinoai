# Nvidia RTX Spark — Nvidia's first PC chip puts 1 petaflop of AI in a laptop

## What Is It
Nvidia unveiled RTX Spark at Computex 2026 (May 31 – June 1), a Grace Blackwell superchip designed to power a new class of Windows AI PCs. This is Nvidia's first entry into the consumer PC processor market — previously Nvidia only made GPUs. RTX Spark combines an Arm CPU + Blackwell GPU on a single chip, bringing data-center-grade AI to thin laptops and compact desktops.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Unveiled: Computex 2026 (May 31–June 1, 2026) + officially partnered with Microsoft at Build 2026 June 2
- AI performance: 1 petaflop (same class as AI servers a few years ago)
- CPU: 20-core Nvidia Grace (Arm-based, designed with MediaTek)
- GPU: Blackwell architecture with 6,144 CUDA cores
- Memory: up to 128GB LPDDR5X unified memory, 300 GB/s bandwidth
- Chip interconnect: NVLink-C2C (same technology used in data centers)
- Partners: Dell, HP, Lenovo, Microsoft Surface, ASUS, MSI, Acer, GIGABYTE
- Products: 30+ laptops and ~10 compact desktops planned for fall 2026
- Surface Laptop Ultra powered by RTX Spark coming fall 2026
- Dell XPS 16 Creator Edition with Tandem OLED + 128GB unified memory
- Price: not announced yet; availability fall 2026
- Context: This directly competes with Qualcomm Snapdragon X after Qualcomm's Windows Arm exclusivity deal expired

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
Until now, Nvidia made the AI chips in data centers and the graphics cards in gaming PCs — separately. RTX Spark merges both into one chip for consumer laptops. This means an everyday laptop will soon have the same AI architecture that runs ChatGPT's servers — inside a thin device with all-day battery life. Persian tech enthusiasts who follow AI will want to know: the laptop you buy this fall might be able to run AI models locally that couldn't fit on any consumer device before.

## The Surprising Angle
128GB of unified memory in a laptop. That's not a typo. Current high-end MacBook Pros top out at 128GB but as a premium option costing thousands extra. RTX Spark makes this standard on a consumer AI PC. 128GB of unified memory is enough to run large language models locally that currently require cloud servers. The era of running your own local AI without the cloud is closer than most people think.
