# Google I/O 2026 — AI Search & Agentic Era

## What Is It
Google's annual developer conference (May 19–20, 2026) where the company announced its most aggressive AI push ever — transforming Search, Android, and its entire product line around Gemini models and agentic AI experiences.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Google Search is getting its biggest upgrade in 25+ years, powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash — now answering longer queries, accepting file attachments, and building custom dashboards for ongoing tasks
- Gemini 3.5 Flash is 4x faster than other frontier models at the same intelligence level, and now outperforms Gemini 3.1 Pro in coding and agentic benchmarks
- Gemini Spark — a new personal AI agent — can take actions on your behalf across apps and your digital life, replacing the "answer questions" model with an "do the work" model
- Google announced over 100 products/updates in a single event, spanning Search, Android XR glasses, YouTube, Gmail, shopping, and cloud
- Android XR smart glasses are arriving this fall in partnership with Gentle Monster, Warby Parker, and Samsung
- Gemini Omni accepts image, audio, video, and text input simultaneously and outputs video — no transcription step needed

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
Every Persian-speaking person who uses Google Search daily is about to experience a fundamentally different product. The shift from "type a query, get links" to "describe your problem, get an agent that keeps working for you" is not a future thing — it's happening now. For marketers, entrepreneurs, and students in the audience, AI Mode in Search changes how information is found, which changes how content needs to be made.

## Surprising / Counterintuitive Angle
The most counterintuitive thing: Google is not just adding AI to Search — it is replacing the 25-year-old Search box model itself. Gemini Spark doesn't answer you and stop; it stays active, monitors sources, and notifies you when something matches your need. That means Google Search is becoming closer to a personal employee than a search engine. Most people don't realize this shift is already live, not coming soon.
