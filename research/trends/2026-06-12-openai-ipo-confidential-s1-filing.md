# OpenAI Confidentially Files for IPO — S-1 Submitted to SEC

## What Is It
OpenAI submitted a confidential draft S-1 filing to the US Securities and Exchange Commission on or around June 10, 2026, formally beginning the process toward a public market debut expected in the second half of 2026. This is the first official step toward OpenAI becoming a publicly traded company.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Filing type: Confidential S-1 submission to SEC (standard pre-IPO step)
- Filed: approximately June 10, 2026 (reported June 11-12)
- Current valuation: Anthropic overtook OpenAI with a $965B Series H — OpenAI's last private valuation was lower
- Revenue trajectory: OpenAI had rapid growth in 2025-2026; 1 billion users reached as of June 2026
- Competitor: Anthropic also filed confidentially for IPO on June 1, 2026
- Context: Both of the top two AI companies filed for IPO within 10 days of each other

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
The two biggest AI companies in the world — OpenAI (ChatGPT) and Anthropic (Claude) — filed for IPO within 10 days of each other. This is the moment AI becomes fully mainstream financial infrastructure. For Persian-speaking tech followers, this marks a turning point: AI is no longer a startup story, it's now a stock market story. The race between ChatGPT and Claude will play out on Nasdaq.

## The Surprising Angle
Anthropic filed for IPO on June 1 and is valued at $965 billion — OpenAI, which created the entire ChatGPT wave, is actually being valued lower. The company that started the AI revolution is not the most valuable AI company going public. Plus: both companies are losing money at massive scale. Wall Street will decide if AI is worth it.
