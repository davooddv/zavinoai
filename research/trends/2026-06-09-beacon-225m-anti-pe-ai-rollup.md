# Beacon: "Anti-PE" AI Roll-Up Raises $225M Series C

## What Is It
Beacon is a Toronto-based holding company that buys small, profitable, niche software businesses — the kind that serve youth sports leagues, campgrounds, manufacturers, and unions — and then rebuilds them from scratch with AI. It raised a $225M Series C on June 9, 2026, pushing total funding past $500M in under two years.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Round size: $225M Series C, announced June 9, 2026
- Investors: General Catalyst (lead), HarbourVest, Lightspeed, Intrepid Growth Partners, BDT & MSD Partners
- Total funding: over $500M in less than two years
- Previous round: $250M Series B in November 2025 (CEO at the time said it would be the last round — raised a larger C just 7 months later)
- Acquisition pace: roughly one small software company per two weeks
- Target companies: under $20M ARR, founder-led, "everyday economy" verticals
- Led by: Nilam Ganenthiran, ex-President of Instacart
- New hires: Mark Schaaf (ex-CTO Instacart, Superhuman) as COO/CPO; Goutham Buchi (ex-AngelList CTO) as CTO
- Source: The Next Web (thenextweb.com/news/beacon-225m-series-c-ai-roll-up), announced June 9, 2026

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
This is a direct demonstration of how AI is being used not just to build new products, but to transform boring, unglamorous existing businesses that nobody pays attention to. For Persian-speaking entrepreneurs and founders, this shows a completely different AI business model: don't build from scratch, buy something already working and supercharge it with AI.

## The Surprising Angle
The CEO of Beacon said in November 2025 that the $250M Series B would be "the company's final round." Seven months later, they raised a larger round ($225M). The growth was so fast the plan changed. Also: Beacon is explicitly positioning itself as the "anti-private equity" firm — PE firms buy companies, cut costs, and sell in 5 years. Beacon buys companies, adds AI, and says it plans to hold them for decades. It's a completely different philosophy of company ownership entering the AI era.
