# China's $295 Billion AI Data Center Plan — Designed to Lock Out Nvidia

## What Is It
China is preparing a 2-trillion yuan ($295 billion) national plan to build a connected grid of AI data centers across the country over the next five years, operated by state firms like China Mobile and China Telecom — with a rule that at least 80% of all chips must be domestic (primarily Huawei), deliberately excluding Nvidia and AMD.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Total budget: $295 billion (2 trillion yuan) over 5 years — Source: Bloomberg, June 9, 2026
- Domestic chip requirement: 80% minimum, primarily Huawei — Source: Bloomberg / The Next Web, June 9-10, 2026
- Target completion: connected national data center grid by 2028
- Operators: China Mobile, China Telecom (state-owned)
- Governing body: National Development and Reform Commission drafting the blueprint
- Part of China's "Six Networks" infrastructure program and 2025-2030 five-year plan
- Goal: AI adoption across healthcare, transportation, and city management

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
This is the most direct signal yet that the AI chip war between the US and China is now a full infrastructure war. China is spending more than the entire GDP of many countries to build a parallel AI universe — one where Nvidia doesn't exist. For Persian-speaking tech followers, this reframes the chip embargo story: instead of a block, it's becoming a catalyst for China's own silicon industry.

## The Surprising Angle
America's chip ban on China was supposed to cripple China's AI ambitions. Instead, it's forcing China to build a $295 billion independent AI infrastructure — 80% domestic chips. Nvidia being banned from the largest data center buildout in history may end up being the thing that creates Nvidia's biggest long-term rival.

The irony: the US export controls that were meant to slow China down may have accelerated China's push to build its own chip ecosystem at national scale.
