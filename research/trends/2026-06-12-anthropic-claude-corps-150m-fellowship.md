# Anthropic Claude Corps — $150M Fellowship to Embed AI in Nonprofits

## What Is It
Anthropic announced on June 11, 2026 a $150 million program called Claude Corps that will pay 1,000 fellows $85,000/year each to work full-time inside US nonprofits for one year, teaching them how to use Claude. No degree required — anyone over 18 with under two years of full-time work experience can apply.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Program total: $150 million committed by Anthropic
- Fellows: 1,000 total across three cohorts
- Pay: $85,000/year salary + benefits + Claude token budget
- Host organizations: 400+ nonprofits across America
- First cohort: 100 fellows, starts October 2026 — applications close July 17
- Operator: CodePath (a computer education nonprofit) serves as employer of record
- Announcement date: June 11, 2026

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
This is a rare case of an AI company investing massively to spread AI literacy beyond tech circles. The $150M commitment, with $85K salaries and zero degree requirements, signals that the AI industry knows it needs to humanize itself — and that there's now a real economic opportunity for young people who learn AI tools, regardless of their academic background. For Persian-speaking audience members considering AI careers, this is a concrete data point: AI skills = $85K salary, no university required.

## The Surprising Angle
A TechTimes headline framed it bluntly: "Claude Corps Fellowship Pays $85K: Anthropic Admits Its AI Will Displace Workers." The program is essentially Anthropic acknowledging the disruption Claude causes and trying to compensate — by paying young people to spread AI adoption into sectors (nonprofits, social services) that would otherwise be left behind. The company that built the tool disrupting jobs is now paying people to help others cope with it.
