# Alphabet raises $80 billion to fund AI infrastructure — Berkshire Hathaway bets $10B

## What Is It
Google's parent company Alphabet announced on June 1, 2026 a plan to raise $80 billion through stock sales to fund AI compute infrastructure. Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway is investing $10 billion of that directly — a significant signal of Wall Street's conviction in Alphabet's AI position.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Total raise: $80 billion in equity offerings (announced June 1, 2026)
- Structure: $30B in underwritten public offerings + $40B in at-the-market program starting Q3 2026
- Berkshire Hathaway: $10B private placement at ~$350/share (split between Class A and Class C stock)
- 2026 capex guidance: $180–190 billion — roughly double 2025's $91.4 billion
- 2027 capex: expected to "significantly increase" compared to 2026
- Google Cloud Q1 2026 revenue: $20 billion — a 63% jump year-over-year
- Google Cloud backlog: $460 billion in contracted future revenue
- Total Big Tech AI capex in 2026: industry estimated at $700 billion
- Google stock dropped on the dilution concern despite the bullish signal

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
This is the clearest sign yet that AI infrastructure is entering a spending war without precedent. Alphabet alone is doubling its infrastructure spend compared to last year — and other tech giants combined are spending $700 billion in 2026 on AI compute. For Persian tech followers, this answers the question: how serious is the AI race? The answer is: $700 billion serious this year alone.

The Berkshire Hathaway angle is the most surprising hook — Warren Buffett, famous for avoiding tech stocks for decades, is now putting $10 billion directly into Google's AI buildout.

## The Surprising Angle
Berkshire Hathaway — Warren Buffett's famously tech-skeptical holding company — just bet $10 billion on Google's AI infrastructure. When the world's most famous value investor starts buying into AI compute, it signals the market has moved from "hype" to "fundamental infrastructure." Also: Alphabet's 2026 capex ($180–190B) is greater than the GDP of many countries.
