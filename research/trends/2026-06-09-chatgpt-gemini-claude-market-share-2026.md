# AI Chatbot War: Gemini Surged 104%, Claude Grew 306%, ChatGPT Still Leads at 54.7%

## What Is It
A new market share report based on Similarweb data (April 2026), published by analytics firm Momentic and covered by TechTimes on June 8, 2026, shows the global AI chatbot traffic landscape has shifted dramatically. ChatGPT still leads but has lost 22 percentage points in 14 months. Gemini nearly quintupled. Claude tripled in a single quarter.

## Key Facts & Stats
- ChatGPT: 54.7% of worldwide AI chatbot web visits (April 2026) — down from 76.5% in February 2025
- Google Gemini: 27.4% — up from 5.6% in February 2025 (growth: ~104% in 6 months)
- Claude (Anthropic): 8.2% — grew 306% in a single quarter (from 203M visits in January 2026 to 824M in April 2026)
- ChatGPT + Gemini together: ~82% of total AI chatbot traffic
- The remaining 5 assistants split roughly 18%
- Data source: Similarweb via Momentic, as of April 2026
- Report coverage: TechTimes, June 8, 2026 (https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318024/20260608/chatgpt-holds-547-ai-chatbot-traffic-claude-306-why-that-number-misleads.htm)
- Also covered: Momentic (https://momenticmarketing.com/blog/top-ai-chatbots), June 2026

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
This directly maps to tools Persian tech users already use. ChatGPT is still king but its grip is loosening fast — and the story of Gemini and Claude's rise is directly tied to Apple's WWDC announcement (1.4 billion iPhones now running Gemini). For anyone building or marketing with AI tools, knowing which platform is growing fastest matters for where to invest time.

## The Surprising Angle
Claude grew 306% in a single quarter — January to April 2026. That's faster than any other major AI chatbot. But the TechTimes analysis warns this number "misleads": Claude's raw visits (824M) are still far below ChatGPT (estimated 4–5B+). The percentage growth is enormous because the base was small. The real story is Gemini: it went from near-zero to 27.4% of the entire market in 14 months, and it's embedded inside 1.4 billion iPhones now — so the ceiling is very high.
