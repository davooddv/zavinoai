# Google Gemini 3.5 Flash — Replaces Gemini 3.1 Pro as Global Search Default

## What Is It
Google upgraded Google Search with Gemini 3.5 Flash as the new default model powering AI Mode for all users globally. Gemini 3.5 Flash is described as achieving frontier-level intelligence at Flash-class speed — matching or beating Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding and agentic benchmarks while being significantly faster and cheaper to run.

## Key Facts & Stats
- Model: Gemini 3.5 Flash — first in a new series combining "frontier intelligence with action"
- Benchmark performance: outperforms Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding and agentic tasks
- Deployment: now the default in Google Search AI Mode, globally, for all users
- Scale: Google Search serves billions of queries per day — this is one of the largest AI model deployments in history
- Companion release: Gemini Omni announced simultaneously — multimodal, video-first model
- Announced at: Google I/O 2026 (late May), rolling out globally through early June 2026
- Also: Google launched Managed Agents API — one API call spins up a full Linux environment for AI agents

## Why It Matters for ZAVINO's Audience
Every time a Persian speaker (or anyone) searches on Google, they are now interacting with Gemini 3.5 Flash. This is not a lab experiment — it is the most widely deployed AI model in human history, by query volume. The upgrade means Google Search is now being run by an AI that beats its previous flagship model on intelligence benchmarks.

## The Surprising Angle
Google's "Flash" tier — which was designed to be the budget, fast model — now outperforms what was previously the flagship "Pro" model. This is the AI industry's equivalent of a budget phone outperforming last year's flagship. The speed vs. intelligence tradeoff is collapsing. Models are getting smarter AND faster AND cheaper at the same time.
